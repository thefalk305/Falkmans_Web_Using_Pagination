After the late 1880's, the majority of Swedish emigrants traveled by boat from Göteborg, Sweden to Hull, England. They would the board a train to Liverpool. From Liverpool the journey continued with the big transatlantic passenger steamers. This is the route that my great grand-father, Nils P. Falkman took when he left Sweden and headed to America. Unlike Nils, my great-grandmother Karin Norén traveled from the village of Arbrä to the port city of Göteborg, then sailed across the sea to Hamburg, Germany. At Hamburg, she boarded a train to Bremen. It was from Bremen that she sailed across the Atlantic Ocean to America.

Here is another map that shows the routes that have been taken by immigrants traveling from Europe to North America. By far, the majority of Swedes came through Göteborg to Hull England, then across England to Liverpool. From Liverpool, they traveled to America.
